Bed maintenance

Tidy beds, healthier plants, and fewer weeds

Modeled after TruGreen’s clear layouts, this page puts the essentials up front: clean edges, weed control, and attentive care for shrubs and flowers.

Service focus

Routine weed removal, debris cleanup, pruning, and edging to keep beds neat and plants thriving.

Best timing

Every 3–4 weeks through the growing season, with seasonal pruning windows for each plant type.

Pairs well with

Mulching and tree & shrub care for moisture retention and healthier roots.

How we maintain beds

Clean, protect, and shape—step by step

1

Inspect & prioritize

Walkthrough to spot weeds, overgrowth, disease risks, and irrigation issues.

2

Weed & clear

Hand-pull or spot-treat weeds, remove debris, and redefine edges for a sharp outline.

3

Prune & feed

Shape shrubs and perennials, then apply pre-emergent or fertilizer as needed.

What to expect

  • Cleaner beds with fewer weeds and fresher edges after each visit.
  • Pruning timed to plant type so blooms and new growth aren’t sacrificed.
  • Recommendations for mulch depth or irrigation tweaks to keep beds thriving.

  • Seasonal color plantings
  • Soil amendments
