Fall lawn recovery guide for thicker spring growth

Restore compacted soil, add seed where summer heat thinned turf, and feed your lawn so it greens up sooner.

By Edwards Landscape Group • Updated September 5, 2024 • 7 min read

  4. Feed and water
  5. Finish with cleanup

Fall is the best time to reset cool-season grasses in Virginia. Soil is still warm enough for quick germination, nights are cooler, and weeds slow down. Following this TruGreen-inspired sequence ensures new seed takes root and existing turf rebounds before winter dormancy.

1. Evaluate summer stress

Walk the yard to spot thin areas, compaction from foot traffic, or drought stress. Note any fungus damage so you can adjust watering and plan fungicide if needed.

2. Aerate compacted soil

Core aeration opens space for roots and improves nutrient uptake. Our crews run cross patterns for even coverage and flag irrigation heads beforehand to protect your system.

3. Overseed bare areas

Spread premium seed immediately after aeration so it can fall into the open cores. We match seed to your sun and shade conditions for even color and density.

  • Shade-tolerant blends for tree-lined yards
  • Sun-loving mixes for open turf
  • Starter fertilizer to boost germination

4. Feed and water

A balanced fertilizer with slow-release nitrogen fuels root growth without forcing tall top growth before winter. Water lightly twice a day for the first two weeks, then reduce to deeper, less frequent cycles.

5. Finish with cleanup

End the season with a leaf cleanup and one final mowing at a slightly lower height. This prevents matting under snow and keeps air moving through the canopy.
