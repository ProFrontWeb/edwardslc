Lawn care 101

Top 5 reasons you need lawn maintenance services

Consistent mowing, edging, and seasonal cleanups keep your turf healthy and make your weekends easier.

By Edwards Landscape Group • Updated March 12, 2024 • 6 min read

Quick navigation

  1. Health benefits
  2. Curb appeal
  3. Time savings
  4. Seasonal protection
  5. Property value

You already know mowing matters, but the timing, height, and cleanup after each visit determine how well your lawn handles heat, drought, and heavy foot traffic. Edwards Landscape Group crews follow agronomic best practices that mirror the TruGreen approach you see nationwide, adapted for Lynchburg's weather and soil.

1. Health benefits that start at the roots

Proper cutting height changes with the season. We adjust blades to keep more leaf surface during summer and lower the deck slightly in spring and fall to stimulate new growth. Paired with sharp blades and alternating mowing patterns, this keeps roots deeper and less prone to disease.

  • Sharp blades that prevent frayed leaf tips and brown edges.
  • Pattern rotation that reduces ruts and soil compaction.
  • Clippings mulched to return nutrients without smothering turf.

2. Curb appeal that stays consistent

Weekly edging around beds, walks, and driveways keeps lines crisp. We finish each visit with debris cleanup so mulch color, paver joints, and driveway edges look fresh—just like the clean visuals in TruGreen's service photos.

Service pairing to make it last

Combine mowing with bed maintenance or mulching to keep weeds down and moisture balanced between turf and plantings.

3. Time savings for busy weeks

You get a predictable route schedule and text reminders before arrival. Crews handle trimming, blowing, and gate security, so you can plan weekends without the chore list.

  • Standard weekly or biweekly visits
  • Rain-delay rescheduling
  • Pet-safe gate checks

4. Seasonal protection that prevents setbacks

Transition seasons are when turf is most vulnerable. Aeration and overseeding in early fall, paired with proper mowing height, set roots before winter. In spring, a cleanup visit removes salt, sticks, and thatch so new blades emerge evenly.

Spring touch-up

Raise the deck and remove winter debris to avoid scalping new growth.

  • Cleanup branches and salt-damaged patches
  • Bed edging ahead of pre-emergent
  • Mulch refresh to lock in moisture

Fall resilience

Core aeration and overseeding paired with steady mowing helps seed establish.

  • Soil breathing room for roots
  • Balanced watering schedule
  • Leaf removal that won't choke turf

5. Property value and peace of mind

A uniform lawn boosts first impressions for guests and buyers. Routine maintenance also catches issues early—like fungus rings or irrigation problems—so we can loop in fertilization or tree and shrub care before damage spreads.
