Mulch color guide: picking the right finish for your beds

Fresh mulch protects plant roots, controls weeds, and sets the tone for your home’s curb appeal.

By Edwards Landscape Group • Updated July 18, 2024 • 5 min read

Quick navigation

  2. Choosing colors
  3. How much to install
  4. When to refresh

TruGreen-style mulching focuses on more than looks: consistent depth protects soil temperature, locks in moisture, and blocks weeds. Use this guide to pick a color and refresh schedule that fits your home and plantings.

1. Why mulch matters

  • Prevents rapid soil temperature swings around roots.
  • Stops sunlight from reaching weed seeds so they stay dormant.
  • Reduces evaporation to keep irrigation costs down.

2. Choosing colors

Natural brown

Great for blending with wooded lots and brick facades.

Classic black

High contrast that makes evergreens and flowering plants pop.

Rich red

Bold color that pairs with lighter siding and stone.

3. How much to install

We recommend a 2–3 inch depth for most beds. Going thicker can suffocate roots, while thinner layers break down too quickly to stop weeds.

4. When to refresh

In Central Virginia, spring installations set you up for summer heat, while a light fall top-off keeps soil insulated through winter.
