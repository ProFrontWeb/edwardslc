Aeration & seeding

Relieve compaction and rebuild thicker turf

Core aeration followed by overseeding opens the soil, lets nutrients in, and fills bare spots so your lawn bounces back dense and healthy.

Best season: Early fall Includes starter fertilizer Flagged utilities before we core
Book aeration View all services

Service focus

Pulling 2–3 inch cores across the yard to relieve compaction and allow air, water, and nutrients into the root zone.

Best timing

Primary window in September–October with optional spring touch-ups if winter stress or traffic thins turf.

Pair with

Seasonal fertilization and weed control to protect new seed while thickening the lawn.

How it works

Three-part aeration & seeding plan

1

Site prep & marking

We flag irrigation heads, invisible fences, and utilities, then mow if needed so tines reach the soil.

2

Deep core aeration

Commercial aerators pull plugs in two directions to open channels and reduce soil compaction.

3

Premium overseeding

We broadcast region-fit fescue/bluegrass blends and add a low-salt starter fertilizer to jumpstart germination.

What to expect

  • Cores remain on the surface and break down within 1–2 weeks as they feed the soil.
  • Water lightly twice daily for the first two weeks, then transition to deeper, less frequent watering.
  • Mow once seedlings reach 3–3.5 inches; we’ll advise on the first cut timing.

Follow-up care

We align your fertilization and weed control so it nurtures new seed instead of stressing it.

  • Starter fertilizer included
  • Pre-emergent scheduled after establishment
  • Soil moisture guidance
